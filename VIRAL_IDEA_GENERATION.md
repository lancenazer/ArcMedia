# VIRAL IDEA GENERATION GUIDE
## ArcMedia Group - Finding Viral Video Concepts

**Last Updated:** 2026-04-08
**Purpose:** Systematic approach to finding viral video ideas that drive channel growth

---

## CORE PRINCIPLE

> "The biggest driver of YouTube success is the idea itself."

Viral growth is NOT random — it comes from repeatedly testing strong ideas, spotting what already works, and packaging it in a highly clickable way.

---

# METHOD 1: Competitor Outlier Analysis

## What It Is
Find videos that achieve **3-50x more views** than the channel average.

## How to Do It
1. Identify channels in your niche
2. Look for videos with 3-50x more views than typical
3. **Focus on last 6 months** — trends change quickly
4. Analyze WHY those specific videos performed

## Application to Our Channels

| Channel | What to Search | Outlier Threshold |
|---------|---------------|-------------------|
| Visionary Builds | MegaBuilds, construction channels | 100K+ views |
| Anchor Watch | Naval/military channels | 50K+ views |
| Film Lore | Entertainment channels | 100K+ views |

## Research Process
- [ ] Identify top 5 competitor channels
- [ ] Analyze last 6 months of uploads
- [ ] Note outlier videos (view count vs average)
- [ ] Extract common elements from outliers

---

# METHOD 2: Content Gap Research

## What It Is
Find questions people are asking but YouTube hasn't covered well yet.

## How to Do It
1. Read comments on popular videos in niche
2. Use **AnswerThePublic** to find unasked questions
3. Check what audience wants but doesn't see

## Tools to Use
- **AnswerThePublic** — Question keyword research
- **TubeBuddy** — Keyword explorer
- **YouTube Comments** — Direct audience feedback
- **Google Trends** — Rising topics

## Application to Our Channels
- Check comments on top videos: What do people want to know?
- Use AnswerThePublic for: "how to", "why does", "what is"
- Gap = opportunity

---

# METHOD 3: "Top 1%" Bold Concepts

## What It Is
Brainstorm "crazy" ideas that feel almost too good to be true, then borrow proven viral structures.

## Proven Viral Structures to Borrow
- "$1 vs $1,000,000" (comparison)
- "I tested one-star [product]" (testing)
- "24 hours in [extreme location]" (challenge)
- "The TRUTH They HID" (exposé)
- "BACK FROM THE DEAD" (comeback)
- "WHO WINS" (competition)

## Volume Approach
- Generate **20-30 ideas at a time**
- Most won't hit — but ONE strong idea raises channel baseline significantly
- Test consistently, spot what works

# PRACTICAL PROCESS FOR OUR WORKFLOW

## Weekly Idea Generation Ritual

### Step 1: Competitor Scan (30 min)
- [ ] Check top 3 channels in each niche
- [ ] Note last 6 months outliers
- [ ] Document what made them work

### Step 2: Comment Research (20 min)
- [ ] Read top 5 videos in niche comments
- [ ] Find repeated questions
- [ ] Note gaps in content

### Step 3: Bold Concept Brainstorm (20 min)
- [ ] Generate 10 ideas using viral structures
- [ ] Prioritize: unique + bold + specific
- [ ] Select top 3 for testing

### Step 4: Title Testing (10 min)
- [ ] Create 4 title variations per idea
- [ ] Apply curiosity gap + specific numbers
- [ ] Pick best based on AIDA principle

# IDEATION CHECKLIST

Before any video is approved, verify:

- [ ] Does it create a burning question?
- [ ] Is there a curiosity gap?
- [ ] Does it use a proven viral structure?
- [ ] Is the title specific (numbers, names)?
- [ ] Does it have competitor outlier potential?
- [ ] Does it fill a content gap?
- [ ] Does thumbnail + title combo build interest?

- Strong outlier potential: pattern analysis, unique takes
- Content gaps: business/industry analysis, why some survive
- Use: pattern analysis, hidden truths, business angle
